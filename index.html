The Lucas Series It is said that the French mathematican, Edouard Lucas (1842-1891), who was fascinated with the Fibonacci number sequence, decided to make a clever pattern of his own by simply modifying the first two numbers.

2, 1, 3, 4, 7, 11, 18, ....

Like the Fibonanci sequence, each number in the series is formed by the sum of the two previous numbers. However, note that first two numbers are 2 and 1 instead of 0 and 1. The Lucas sequence has a great deal of interesting patterns, the most interesting of which is its close relationship to the golden ratio!
